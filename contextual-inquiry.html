Consolidating Communication


Contextual inquiry & consulting for two museums.

A UX project conducted in coordination with Chance Murphy, Laima Augustaitis, and Youngmin Kim.
Role: user researcher, interviewer, editor

Project Description

My team and I worked with a local recreational nature center (Client A) and applied sciences museum (Client B) to provide suggestions on how to streamline communications for their recently merged organization. As part of a graduate course on Consulting Foundations & Contextual Inquiry, our approach to the problem followed several processes such as background research, contextual inquiry and interviews, affinity diagrams, and qualitative analysis.

The Problem


Client A and Client B recently merged, but have yet to consolidate their respective software and communication suites: Client A operates on Google's G Suite, and Client B on Microsoft's Office 365. The two platforms are not compatible with each other and this mismatch of systems has lead to significant challenges regarding emailing, scheduling, and calendaring. Furthermore, employees at both locations are experiencing inertia, resulting in resistance to change.
