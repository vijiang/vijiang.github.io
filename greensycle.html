GreenSycle

An interaction design project.
Role: User researcher, UX/UI designer, prototyper

With this design project, my goal was to address the problems and incorrect recycling practices that can arise when the information and regulations of Ann Arborâ€™s curbside recycling program are not well known. I hope to better inform residents of what can and cannot be recycled, and to also suggest means for the collective recycling of items that are not able to be placed in convenient curbside bins (such as plastic bags, thermal papers, and certain types of packing materials).

Problem Statement:


Prompt: Think of a design solution that can improve your community or day-to-day life.

In a recent article for the New York Times, Livia Albeck-Ripka describes a behavior that waste managers have begun to call wishful or "aspirational recycling," which occurs when "we want something to be recyclable, so we put it in the recycling bin." This unintentional sabotage of recycling initiatives is quite detrimental, as contaminated or impure batches of recycled materials are often rejected by buyers and sent to the landfill instead.
