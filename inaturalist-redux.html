iNaturalist Application Redesign

Role: UI/UX Designer

iNaturalist is a platform for recording and sharing observations of biodiversity around the world. I redesigned several screens from its mobile application for a course project.

Design Critique

Goals & Audience

Users contribute by taking photos of plants or animals around them and uploading them to the application. Next, a user can either identify the subject themselves or rely on the application to assist them. Other users can then comment to correct or verify the ID.

The target audience can be loosely segmented into two groups: the first is composed of professional scientists, naturalists, and researchers; the second includes environmental hobbyists and those who take a strong interest in learning about the natural world around them.

Main Points

Overall, the current design strives for simplicity and efficiency. Users may be in a rush to post or identify an observation on-the-go, which it is impotant that items are clear and easily accessible. With this in mind, it could be improved in the following areas:

  1. Text is often small and difficult to read; the latter comes from insufficient contrast when text is layered over photos of biodiversity, which are often composed of several different colours.
  2. Clickable items are small and cramped; following Fitt’s law, they could be made larger, with adequate spacing separating them.
  3. It is not clear what clicking does for some items; there is not enough description or detail to indicate what will happen as a result of touching an item.
  4. Images are difficult to identify when sized as small as they currently are.
