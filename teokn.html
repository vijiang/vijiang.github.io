Transportation Equity Open Knowledge Network (TEOKN)

Role: UI/UX Designer, User Researcher

Summary

The Transportation Equity Open Knowledge Network — “TEOKN” — is a knowledge network that integrates datasets and enables peer learning.

In my role, I worked with a team of professionals to create interactive website mockups in Figma for the Phase I Prototype of the project.

Initial Research

Stakeholders from both the public and private sectors were interviewed. We were interested in understanding how they used transportation data and what challenges they experienced in doing so.

These observations were collected and organized into an affinity chart, then synthesized into user personas which were used to inform our platform design.

Prototype Designs

In order to develop these screens, we first created basic wireframes with layouts inspired by research into websites in related fields, such as the Shared Mobility Use Center and the Open Data Network.

Using observations from the initial design research, I helped create, ideate, and refine our visual designs and interactions in order to craft a finalized prototype for demonstration at the NSF Expo.

This is a brief demonstration of the network's BigMap feature.

Additionally, I created site wireflows to detail navigation and functional pathways throughout the prototype, to be used for user-facing demonstrations and further testing.

Currently, I am working with a small team of developers to align the public-facing site to our prototypes.

This project is sponsored by the National Science Foundation and is a partnership between the Gerald R. Ford School of Public Policy and the University of Michigan Transporation Research Institute.
